# Quality Rules — Report Drafting Tool

**What this is:** the rules the Numbers Analyst and the Writer must follow so the draft is trustworthy. Two uses: (1) paste into the helpers' instructions, (2) Person B's checklist — every rule below is something to check in a draft before we trust it.

**The one-line spirit:** the tool's job is to be *right and honest*, not to sound confident. A blank we have to fill in is fine. A confident wrong number is fatal — one of those in a leadership report and the tool is dead.

Examples below use our own initiatives (numbered as in the Annual Plan) so the rules are concrete.

---

## A. Numbers you can trust

**1. Never invent a number.** Every figure must trace to the actuals sheet or to a quoted message. If it can't be sourced, write `[number needed]` — never a plausible guess.

**2. Every number carries its comparator.** A figure alone isn't analysis. State what it's measured against — vs plan, vs last month, vs target.
> Not "approval rate was 67%." → "Approval rate 67%, flat vs plan and vs last month."

**3. When sources disagree on a number, show both — never silently pick or average.** If a partner email quotes 35% approval and the actuals sheet says 30%, surface both with attribution and flag it for verification.

---

## B. Cause and effect — the analytical core

**4. "Shipped" ≠ "worked."** State an initiative's *status* and its *metric movement* as separate facts. Never claim a metric moved because an initiative shipped unless the actuals AND the Tracker link support it.
> Initiative 1 (new partner) may be "piloting" while eligible base hasn't moved yet — report both, don't merge them into "partner live, eligible base up."

**5. Attribute only what the data supports, and only to a linked initiative.** Credit an initiative for the part of the promise the numbers actually show — not the whole promise.
> Initiative 5 promised eligible base +5% AND approval rate +10%. If actuals show AR up but eligible base flat, credit the AR gain to it and explicitly say the eligible-base expansion did *not* land.

**6. Trace top-line misses through the funnel — don't hand-wave.** When disbursement or any headline number misses, follow it to the stage that actually moved, and name the initiative responsible.
> Initiative 9 lifted ticket size and submission rate but approval rate fell 3% (users applying for bigger loans get rejected). The tool must compute the *net* disbursement effect through the funnel, not assume "ticket up = good."

**7. Distinguish real movement from mechanical movement.** If a number moved because of a definition or formula change rather than real performance, say so plainly.

**8. Respect timing (lead/lag).** An initiative that landed late in the month may not show impact yet. Write "shipped late-month, impact not yet measurable" — not "no impact."

---

## C. Promise vs reality — mandatory

**9. For every initiative with a number promise, state expected vs realized.** Including "not yet realized" or "not yet measurable." Partial delivery is reported as partial.
> Initiative 5 → "Expected: eligible +5%, AR +10%. Realized: AR +10% achieved; eligible expansion 0% — partner's Gini came in below expectation and their profit target absorbed the gain. Half-delivered."

---

## D. Honesty about uncertainty

**10. Keep tentative things tentative.** "We might push to September" is a *risk*, not a fact. Carry the confidence level (High / Medium / Low) through from the Collector into the draft.

**11. Separate fact, inference, and recommendation** so a reader can tell which is which. Facts come from data or messages; inferences are the tool's reasoning; recommendations are judgment. Don't dress an inference as a confirmed fact.

**12. Put everything unconfirmed into an explicit "To verify" list, per owner.** Don't smooth an uncertainty into confident prose to make the draft read better. The open-questions list is a feature, not a failure.

---

## E. Completeness and the forward-looking sections

**13. Account for every Tracker initiative.** Done → a closing line. In progress / delayed / blocked → a status line. Silent on something → an open question. Nothing disappears without a trace.

**14. Don't invent initiatives.** If the comms mention something not in the Tracker, flag it as a *candidate new initiative* for review — never write it in as established fact.

**15. Forecasts must state their basis.** Next-month run-rate (Section 2) is anchored on the plan, adjusted for known slips — and says so. Do not bake the promised impact of a *delayed* initiative into next month's number.
> Initiative 2 is delayed ~2 months → its eligible +10% must NOT appear in next month's run-rate.

**16. Top-priority selection follows a stated rule, not vibes.** Priorities = high promised-impact initiatives that are delayed, blocked, or at-risk.
> Initiative 2 (high impact, now delayed) and Initiative 7 (blocked — FP&A won't fund the costly channel) are the natural priorities/escalations to surface.
